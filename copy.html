My Horological Story

My fascination with watches began as a child, watching my father wind his old Seiko. Years later, I see watches as more than just tools—they are wearable art and feats of micro-engineering.

Each watch in my collection marks a chapter in my life. From the affordable Casio that survived school days to the vintage Omega I restored myself, every piece brings back memories and lessons in patience, craftsmanship, and style.

Watch Life
As artificial intelligence continues to reshape the job market, I believe it’s important to become a well‑rounded individual — someone who is adaptable, versatile, and eager to learn across disciplines. This website reflects that philosophy. It showcases my projects, my creativity in marketing through portrait editing, my communication skills through blogging, and my ability to present and sell my skillset to future clients.
While volunteering in Namibia to help restore the habitat of desert elephants, I saw how heat and environmental stress affect both wildlife and communities. That experience drives me to focus on sustainable engineering that makes a real impact.
I am making it a practice to say yes to opportunities, as I am convinced that each individual and experience has something to teach me. My focus will be on continuous growth, I’ll remain open to new perspectives and challenges, because I believe that mindset is the key to unlocking my full potential.
Complete the WMM Seven Star Journey by running all seven iconic marathons: Tokyo, Boston, London, Berlin, Chicago, New York and Sydney. This journey is not only about the runs — it reflects the same qualities I bring to life: long‑term commitment, resilience, and consistent effort.
I usually recharge by running to prepare the upcoming marathon and spending time outdoors. Lately, I’ve also been experimenting with cooking dishes from different countries, which has been a fun way to share meals with friends and a practical alternative to eating out during this cost‑of‑living crisis.
